Flying
I earned my private pilot's license in 2006 using money saved from my Iraq deployment and a fantastic scholarship given to me by Peggy Ogram in Yuma, Arizona. I learned to fly with Jim McDermott at Somerton Airport, where Columbia astronaut Dr. Laurel Clark also learned to fly. I'm currently building cross-country hours so I can begin training for my IFR and Commercial ratings. My heart's set on building a little snub-nosed Sonex once I've got a regular income and can trade my one-bedroom apartment for an actual house with a garage to work in.

Member of EAA since 2005; member of chapter 590 in Yuma, Arizona and built their website: eaa590.org This chapter's really active in building planes and organizing Saturday flyouts for breakfast to neighboring airports and Wednesday night-flights to keep current. The chapter is mostly based at Yuma International and Somerton Airports. I made the Somerton site: somertonairport.com

Member of the Ninety-Nines, an international organization of women pilots. The Imperial So-Lo chapter in El Centro, California, welcomed me with open arms. I'm now in the Columbia Cascade 99s in Portland, and have built their website as well: columbiacascade99s.org

I'm really interested in the history of aviation, particularly 1900 to the 1950s. I collected and scanned the images from the Yuma Endurance Days celebration of the then-world record endurance flight by Bob Woodward and Woody Jongeward that put Yuma, Arizona on the aviation map.
