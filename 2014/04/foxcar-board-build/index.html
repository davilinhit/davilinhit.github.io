Foxcar Board Build

Just a quick update to say the Foxcar Control Board is built! I left the ENABLE pin unconnected on the voltage regulator (this is what I get for not seeking out a design review…) so I had to short that pin to VCC. With that done, plugging into the USB successfully illuminated the 3.3V and VIN LEDs, so power is theoretically available to both the STM32F4 and the motor driver chip.

I did tear off the microUSB when trying to disconnect the USB cable so I resolve from now on to always choose USB packages with anchoring slots. I had to RTV it down, resolder, and then just ripped it up again so I gave up and switched over to providing power from the 7V LiPo directly.

Turns out that worked fine too, at least at first, so the LEDs for VIN and 3.3V were lit as expected but I definitely had a short from 3.3V to GND somewhere and I made a little volcano in the middle of my STM32F4 chip.

While I wait to get a chance to rework, including remove/replacing that chip, I’m going back to prototyping on the STM32F4 Discovery board.
