Exposure Example

February 2014 - Tualatin, Oregon

This is an exposure example using an X100S in snow at Tualatin Wildlife Refuge on Sunday, February 9th, 2014.

I wanted to get a sense for ISO, aperture, exposure time, and brightness of the resulting image when stepping from -2 to +2 EV exposure bias with the mode on auto. I didn’t control for aperture. I didn’t control for anything, really, I’m just playing around. The results make sense because as the shutter is open longer, more light is captured.

ISO: 1/950s
Brightness: 8.94
Aperture: F11
Shutter Speed: 1/969s = 1.03 milliseconds
Exposure Bias: -2 EV

ISO: 1/850s
Brightness: 8.79
Aperture: F8
Shutter Speed: 1/873s = 1.14 milliseconds
Exposure Bias: -1 EV

ISO: 1/900s
Brightness: 8.83
Aperture: F5.7
Shutter Speed: 1/891s = 1.12 milliseconds
Exposure Bias: 0 EV

ISO: 1/640s
Brightness: 9.37
Aperture: F5.7
Shutter Speed: 1/648s = 1.54 milliseconds
Exposure Bias: +1 EV

ISO: 1/450s
Brightness: 9.87
Aperture: F5.7
Shutter Speed: 1/458s = 2.18 milliseconds
Exposure Bias: +2 EV
