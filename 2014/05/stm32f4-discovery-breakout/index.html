STM32F4-Discovery Breakout Board

May 2014 - Portland, Oregon

The STM32F4-Discovery board is a really great dev board that breaks out all the pins to, unfortunately, male headers. All of my sensors have male pins so I finally gave up daisy-chaining female-to-male jumpers and decided to make a breakout board on the LPKF router in the Electronics Prototyping Lab.

I was lucky enough to find Jason Lopez’s Eagle schematic and footprint but I had trouble with the drills so I changed the drill size. Otherwise it saved me a ton of time and effort. Thanks, Jason!

This is the schematic and board layout for the two-layer board. It’s really a one-layer board, as the vias aren’t plated. I ran all the traces on the bottom side of the board so I could place the female headers through from the top.

There are four female headers so the dev board doesn’t have to be soldered in and four more for the jumpers to attach.
