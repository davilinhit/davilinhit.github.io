City of Yuma’s Endurance Days

In 1949, Bob Woodward and Woody Jongeward flew an Aeronca Sedan called the City of Yuma non-stop for 47 days in a successful attempt to showcase the great flying weather and convince aviation to stick around in Yuma, Arizona, after the war.
Friends of Bob and Woody located the plane in Minnesota in 1997 and brought it home to Yuma to be restored; both Bob and Woody were were present as it was flown briefly for a fiftieth anniversary re-enactment flight.
