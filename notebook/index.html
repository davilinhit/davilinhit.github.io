Wednesday, December 25 2013

This note is trying out my new script. It first gets the current date in the right format.

NAME=$(date +"%Y-%m-%d")

Then it copies a template I made in _layouts/ and names it using that date output.

cp $SRCDIR/_layouts/note_ .textile $SRCDIR/_posts/$NAME-note.textile

Then it opens it for editing so I can do my thing.

vim $SRCDIR/_posts/$NAME-note.textile

And here it is. Yay for lowering transaction costs.

I also figured out a way to add image to posts but they only show up on the notebook page, not the RSS feed, because they’re slid in from the yaml header and sit above the title. They look nice so I’m calling it good.

Tuesday, December 10 2013

Hahahaha, and then I didn’t write at all again for three weeks.

I enjoyed the holiday and I’ve been hacking away at this Foxcar control board. I’ve started journaling in a real actual physical journal and I’ve found it helpful to not have an audience to worry about.

The RF front-end board schematic is done, though I’m still a little unhappy with the power section, but there’s another PSAS member who’s working on a separate board that’s also set up for the same power source so if he arrives at a nicer solution before I get the layout finished (I’m starting from the RF side) then I’ll change my choice of chips.

I’m unhappy because the current switching power supply is a 60-pin LGA so you can’t get to the traces once it’s been soldered to the board. I’m ordering the board through OSHPark then reflow it so probably everything will be fine but I still get worried about parts I only get one chance with.

Also, it’s expensive, but it’s designed to handle 20V on the rocket Power over Ethernet bus so I guess you get what you pay for. I have a micro-USB connector for at-laptop debug.

Annnd the Foxcar control board schematic is done and I pushed to github. I started on the layout, started the trace calculations, and then reinstalled Starcraft II. I can’t stop thinking about playing it. I think I might be slightly burned out…

Tonight I cleaned up little things on the website that were bothering me, including the Endurance Days part, and now I’m going to go home and hunt me some Zerg.

Drove over to Cape Lookout on the coast for the morning. Low fog in the valleys and it’s finally tilted over into cold. Turned the heat on when I realized the cats were cuddling a little more desperately than normal.

I’ve focused on work and BJJ this week but I bought the Pololu VNH5019 motor driver shield which can handle over 10 A for a single motor. I figured out the wiring I’ll need for the Foxcar once that gets here. I’m probably going to do a custom board with:

- XBee wireless (need to explore RF transceivers – I know nothing about RC hobby RF stuff, except that there’s a ton of it out there and has been forever.

- camera that’s fast enough for real-time analysis, so I had to discard a couple I found through Adafruit and Sparkfun.

- 9DOF Sparkfun sensor board

- micro SD card for data logging or image logging

- micro-USB and JTAG

Actually, putting a Node 6 from PSAS on it might be a good idea.
