Sunday, November 3 2013

Drove over to Cape Lookout on the coast for the morning. Low fog in the valleys and it’s finally tilted over into cold. Turned the heat on when I realized the cats were cuddling a little more desperately than normal.

I’ve focused on work and BJJ this week but I bought the Pololu VNH5019 motor driver shield which can handle over 10 A for a single motor. I figured out the wiring I’ll need for the Foxcar once that gets here. I’m probably going to do a custom board with:

- an STM32F4 processor

- XBee wireless (need to explore RF transceivers – I know nothing about RC hobby RF stuff, except that there’s a ton of it out there and has been forever.

- camera that’s fast enough for real-time analysis, so I had to discard a couple I found through Adafruit and Sparkfun.

- USB

- JTAG mini

Actually, putting a Node 6 from PSAS on it might be a good idea. I’ll have to think about it.

Saturday, October 26 2013

Still foggy but the afternoons have been clear blue sky.

I drove in to downtown to hack with greh at Portland State. Started out by putting Raspbian on my Raspberry Pi. I’ve exhausted Wicker Man references for my servers so on greh’s advice, I’m calling it rasputin. At least this way, I should be unable to kill it.

I picked up the IMEX Firefox that showed up in the lab and ripped the guts out, replacing the IMEX control board with an Arduino and Adafruit motor shield. The motor clicks but I swapped servos as well and that works great.

My cat has decided that waking at 3am and show me affection by licking my head until I get out of bed is an A++ way to contribute to a happy and functioning household. I’m not sure how to convince my cat that it’s pretty fucking wrong.

Thursday, October 24 2013

Still foggy.

I’m still having some forearm pain so I skipped BJJ and came home to figure out how to break the reading-of-articles that I want to do away from my compulsive browsing-of-Reddit. I have a wifi-only, first-gen iPad for reading and a first-gen (I think) Kindle for reading on backpacking/hiking trips. I added Instapaper to my Firefox on all my computers (work, home desktop, laptop) and dropped all pending bookmarks in there.

I’m using Trello for a general tracking-of-everything and I don’t want this to be a part of that at all. I also can email PDFs to myself to get them into the Kindle App. That should be enough to make this stuff available to me. And I can save links from Ars or Reddit or whatever for later, without wasting cycles worrying that I’ll never see it again.

Hopefully this’ll help clear my head. I have a pile of autonomous quadcopter papers I want to read.

I posted the photos from building my 38mm Sentinel rocket from Binder Designs.

I made lemon poppyseed scones that were actually pretty amazing. Also have discovered lemon curd.
