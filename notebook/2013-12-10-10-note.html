I enjoyed the holiday and I’ve been hacking away at this Foxcar control board. I’ve started journaling in a real actual physical journal and I’ve found it helpful to not have an audience to worry about.

The RF front-end board schematic is done, though I’m still a little unhappy with the power section, but there’s another PSAS member who’s working on a separate board that’s also set up for the same power source so if he arrives at a nicer solution before I get the layout finished (I’m starting from the RF side) then I’ll change my choice of chips.

I’m unhappy because the current switching power supply is a 60-pin LGA so you can’t get to the traces once it’s been soldered to the board. I’m ordering the board through OSHPark then reflow it so probably everything will be fine but I still get worried about parts I only get one chance with.

Also, it’s expensive, but it’s designed to handle 20V on the rocket Power over Ethernet bus so I guess you get what you pay for. I have a micro-USB connector for at-laptop debug.
