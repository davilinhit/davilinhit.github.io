Hi, I’m Jenner. I’m an electrical engineer who designs Enhanced Vision Systems, which are blended infrared/visible aircraft cameras which see at night and through smoke, haze, and light fog. I live and work around Portland, Oregon.

I’m on LinkedIn and my open source code is on GitHub.

You can email me at jeh.wicker@gmail.com.

I’m mainly interested in building hardware and firmware to support open source science though sensor packages and mesh nets for data collection, particularly in remote or hostile environments.

Eventually, I’d like to throw robots at other planets.

I’m currently writing and building open source hardware and software for robots, cameras, amateur rockets, and my garden. I’ve helped build homebuilt experimental aircraft and I’m interested in open source avionics.

My first languages are C and VHDL but I’m proficient in C++, Assembly, Verilog, Python, and Matlab. I use Eagle, Spice, DxDesigner, OSHPark, SolidWorks, a laser cutter, a 3d printer, and an LPKF router to prototype my hardware designs.

I’m part of the Portland State Electronics Prototyping Lab and Aerospace Society.

When I’m not making stuff, I’m into hiking, photography, astronomy, and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. I have two awesome cats.
