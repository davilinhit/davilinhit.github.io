Hi!

You can find my code and background info at GitHub and LinkedIn.
Occasionally I remember to post on Google+.

If you want to actually make contact, please use email at jeh.wicker@gmail.com.

I’m an avionics team member of the Portland State Aerospace Society buillding sophisticated amateur high-power rockets.

I’m a geek in my spare time, too. I have my ham radio license (KF7VJX) and I’m a private pilot. I like traveling, hiking, reading, writing, and being a lowly white belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu at Five Rings in Beaverton, Oregon.

Professionally I’m an electrical engineer in product development doing design-build-test on hardware and software to make enhanced vision systems, which are blended visible and infrared cameras for general aviation aircraft. I design PCBs, assemble and test prototypes, troubleshoot hardware, and happily code in C/C++ and VHDL.

I’m a veteran Marine who spent five years as an aircraft electrician working on AV8-B Harriers, which took me to Iraq in 2006 and to sea on the USS Tarawa in 2008. I was a nineteen-year old kid when I enlisted and my political views have been shaped by my experience; please don’t assume anything about them.

My independent research interests are a blend of aircraft, autonomous vehicles, robot motion planning, low-cost embedded systems, and planetary science. Someday I want to work on rovers to explore the solar system. I’m a big fan of finding ways to build hardened and robust systems with the idea that you could send one hundred $1,000 robots instead of one $100,000 robot. I accept that moving faster, using cheaper parts, and embracing the idea of rapid prototyping means some percentage of those hundred will be dead on arrival — but that’s OK, that’s why I sent extra.

I made this site with Jekyll. It’s served from Github.
